Why Choose Birmingham?

Birmingham is a great place to live for many reasons. The cost of living is less than the National Average at 93%, even though it is one of the larger metro areas in the south. It is ranked as the 2nd best place to live in Alabama, only behind Huntsville, AL.

One of the things that makes Birmingham such a special place it its history. Birmingham has one of the most notable histories for landmark events in the Civil Rights Movement. There is the incredible Birmingham Civil Rights Institute (BCRI), which houses a direct link to much of this history. Across the street from the BCRI is Bethel Baptist Church, one of the prominent sites of the Birmingham Civil Rights Trail. The Civil Rights Trail has many locations through Birmingham you can follow and see locations that are called "Sites of Violence, Sites of Healing" by the Civil Rights Trail organizers.

Aside from being historically rich, Birmingham is the home of the Alabama Symphony Orchestra and the Birmingham Barons, a minor league baseball team in the Chicago White Sox franchise. There is also a wonderful art museum with free admission that houses many great works of art in its permanent collection and rotating exhibits. With the allure of such attractions in the area as the Birmingham Zoo and the McWane Science Center, Birmingham gives families the attractions of urban living at a cost of living only found in the south.

Aside from the historical and cultural draws, Birmingham is also becoming a place for burgeoning tech companies to make their home. Shipt, Inc, has a major headquarters here in Birmingham, but that isn't all. Birmingham was listed as one of the top cities emerging as tech hotspots in the nation in 2019 along with Huntsville, AL.
